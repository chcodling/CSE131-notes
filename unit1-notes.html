  Instructor Communication and Office Hours

This section contains information and suggestions for you to consider as Unit 1 progresses. It is divided by weeks. See the Student Interactions section of General Teaching Notes for how to maintain an instructor presence in the course throughout the week. Click to expand each weekly section.

Week 01: Course Introduction and CSS Review

Student Communication

  • During Week 01:
    • Make sure students know how to navigate to MS Teams.
    • Make sure students have a GitHub repository for their assignments.
    • Reaffirm that attending your office hour session will be supremely valuable to students as the semester progresses. 
  • Week 02 Announcement:
    • Post your announcement for Week 02 by Saturday evening of Week 01.
    • Preview the Mission Statement II assignment instructions. Let students know how you will be assessing them on their submission.
    • Announce your Office Hour time for Week 02.

Office Hour Suggestions

  • Don't forget to record your session.
  • Consider asking students to share their experience with CSS. Small talk is okay too.
  • Field questions from students on their coursework. Prompt them by asking what they understand about the Mission Statement I assignment.
  • If no students are joining your session, or you are finished fielding questions, proceed to share your screen and work through the W01 Ponder activities, sharing tips, tricks, and insights from your own experience with the topics.
  • Upon conclusion of your office hour, be sure to post a link to the recording in both Teams chat and via a Canvas announcement.

Week 02: JavaScript Introduction

Student Communication

  • During Week 02:
    • Continue to monitor MS Teams and encourage students to both ask questions and help each other.
    • Identify students who did not submit Mission Statement I and reach out to them about their missing submission.
  • Week 03 Announcement:
    • Post your announcement for Week 03 by end-of-week W02.

Office Hour Suggestions

  • Follow up with students on their W01 assignments. Field and answer questions.
  • Ask students whether they prefer using light or dark modes on their computers and in their browsers. Ask why.
  • If no students are joining your session, or you are finished fielding questions, proceed to share your screen and provide a demonstration/walk-through of any of the following:
    • Show appropriate HTML document syntax.
    • Write examples of the CSS code they should have completed for Mission Statement I.
    • Do a walk-through of any of the W02 Ponder activities.
    • Sharing tips, tricks, and insights from your own experience with this weeks topics.
  • Don't forget to post a link to the recording of your office hour for students to watch.

  Grading/Feedback

The following excerpts from the Mission Statement II instructions may be helpful in providing feedback to students.

HTML Modifications

  • "Add a select element to your HTML page."
  • "Add the class of dark to the body element."
  • "Review the...[HTML Select Element article from Mozilla...] for help with the syntax." (This implies specific expectations for element and attribute syntax)

Dark Theme Styling

  • "Start the dark theme with the following changes: set the background color to #333, a nice dark grey. Then, set the font color to white."
  • "Check to see what option is currently selected on our theme selector." (This implies a requirement for the theme to change based on user selection)
  • "The last thing that we need to look at is the logo. It is the same blue that the subtitle was, and so it has the same contrast issues." (This implies a need for a different logo specifically for the dark theme)

JavaScript Functionality

  • "With JavaScript select the 'select` element out of the DOM."
  • "Add an event listener to the selected element. We should listen for a change event."
  • "Create a function called changeTheme that will get called by the event listener when the select option is changed." (These statements detail the expected use of JavaScript and specific event handling)
  • "If it is "dark" then add the dark class to body and change the logo image src to the white logo.<br>If it is not "dark" then remove the dark class from the body element and change the logo image src for the logo to the blue logo." (These outline the actions expected during theme change)
